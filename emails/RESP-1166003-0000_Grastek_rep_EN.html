* Two parallel, Canada-wide structured telephone interviews conducted in 2006 surveying 1,001 allergic rhinitis (AR) adult patients (≥18 years old) and 160 physicians who treat AR. Eligible patients were those suffering from AR as defined by physician diagnosis of AR, nasal allergies or “hay fever”; or use of prescription or over-the-counter (OTC) medications to treat self-diagnosed nasal congestion or symptoms (unrelated to cold or flu). AR patients and physicians were asked questions (adapted from Allergies in America study questionnaire) about AR diagnosis, symptoms, comorbidities, quality of life impact and treatment as well as attitudes toward the disease, expectations for treatment and educational needs. An analysis was performed of symptom control in the worst month in the subgroups of AR patients using OTC vs. prescription medication.1
